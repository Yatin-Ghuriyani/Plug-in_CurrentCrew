Research-Backed Mobility

Our prototype architecture is heavily inspired by cutting-edge IEEE research in smart grids, EV scheduling, and transportation networks. Here is our roadmap.

📚

IEEE Inspired Architecture Guidance

While our current MVP successfully demonstrates the core discovery and booking workflow, our database and backend architecture have been structured to eventually incorporate the following advanced concepts derived from recent IEEE publications:

Queue Optimization

Dynamically distributing EV loads across multiple stations in a geographic radius to minimize collective wait times and prevent grid congestion at single nodes.

EV Charging Scheduling

Smart slot allocation algorithms that balance user convenience with real-time grid load constraints, ensuring stable power distribution.

Demand Forecasting

Utilizing historical booking data to predict peak charging hours, paving the way for dynamic pricing models that incentivize off-peak charging.

🚀

Future Scope Timeline

As outlined in our project presentation, the roadmap for Plug-in includes transitioning from standard booking workflows to fully ML-driven systems.

Phase 1 (Current)

Interactive Discovery

Real-time mapping, live slot tracking, and guaranteed booking reservations using MongoDB.

Phase 2 (Upcoming)

Battery-Aware Routing

Routing users to the optimal station based not just on distance, but ensuring the vehicle can reach it with its current state of charge (SoC).

Phase 3 (Vision)

Dynamic Pricing Engine

Adjusting per-kWh rates in real-time based on local grid demand and station congestion levels to auto-balance the network.
